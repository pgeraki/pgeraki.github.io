Monument to Sunken Utopias
An exercise in digital form

The intent of this exercise was to ‘sculpt’ a plastic form that could assume an entirely different formal identity when perceived from different points of view. The formal language of plasticity was deliberately deployed to allow for a seamless transition from one mode of perception to another. The title “monument to sunken utopias” suggests that the project has no objecthood or identity outside of the particular context in which it finds itself, the particular ideology whose swan-song it represents. In this series of renderings water has been deployed as a metaphor for the prenatal generation of form as well as for its reincarnation through representation.
The project was rendered with minimal means, making use only of a reflective skydome to which different images were applied to create different color effects and lighting conditions. The resulting images were further tampered with to create more unrealistic effects.

Type of Project: Formal / Representational Exercise
