Center for Computing and the Arts
Interactive Contingencies

Interdisciplinary research is by definition contingent, interactive, creative. The Yale Center for Computing and the Arts aspires at such interactive contingencies by combining a rigorous research component and a performance/exhibition component. By setting up a simple dialogue between an enclosed core of highly specific program and a pubic promenade which surrounds it, this proposal rethinks the intersection between the computational sciences and the arts and their respective constituencies. A continuous circuitous void through the entire building allows for visual connections that reinforce the contingency of user interactions. The transition from the building exterior to the interior occurs gradually through the crossing of three successive layers of enclosure, which, in turn, create programmatic 'buffer' zones.
In an obstinate response to the variability of the programmatic requirements for Yaleâ€™s new Center for Computing and the Arts, this design proposes a centrally organized building that continually reinforces and disrupts the power of its center. The public promenade, consisting of exhibition and performance spaces, spirals into the building as an extrusion of the landscape in a rotating motion countered by the motion of the other main circulatory route through the building. Disparate programmatic elements are brought together by the presence of a solid core consisting of highly conditioned spaces that gets voided selectively and treated in different materials to throw off the static and overpowering hierarchy of the alleged center.
