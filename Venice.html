Venice's Island
(In collaboration with Aidan Doyle)

Venice is a city that resists the passing of time and, in doing so, postpones the inevitable encounter with its own mortality. Drawing from Alvise Cornaro's sixteenth century proposal to reinvent the image of the Serenissima by placing an island and an ancient theater within the bacino of San Marco, this project utilizes the figure of the island and its symbolic significance for the history of Venice in order to radically restructure the city through a relatively small-scale intervention.
Venice's newest island, a monumental plinth placed at the heart of the city on the Grand Canal in lieu of the highly symbolic Rialto bridge, functions as a sign of its own impermeability. The choice of material, white Istrian stone, heightens the appearance of massiveness while also referencing the Palladian tradition in Venice. The boundedness and self-contained nature of the object make it an alien presence in a city that integrates everything pertaining to the now. At the same time, however, the island persistently denies its own objecthood by carrying undertones of its precedents. A mirror of Venice's self-constructed image and thus immune to assimilation, the island begins to reformulate Venice from the inside out.

Type of Project: Design Provocation
Location: Venice, Italy
Completion Date: December 2009
